Microprocessor controlled devices are often used in the home, such as a smart thermostat in an oven. Explain how the smart thermostat regulates the oven's temperature. [6 marks]

  • First, the thermostat takes a temperature reading regularly.

  • The analog data from the sensor is converted to digital data using ADC.

  • The values are sent to the microprocessor.

  • The value is compared to a preset range by the microprocessor.

  • If the value of the digital data is higher than the preset value, the microprocessor sends a signal to the oven to turn it off/lower temperature

  • If the value of the digital data is lower than the preset value, the microprocessor sends a signal to the oven to continue heating.

  • ^ All the stuff mentioned above is a control application, which is used in smart sensors.
